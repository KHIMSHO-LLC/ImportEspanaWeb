## Importing a Car from Italy to Spain: Your Practical Guide to a Seamless Transition

Spain, with its stunning landscapes, vibrant culture, and welcoming expat communities, calls to many. For those making the move or already settled, the idea of bringing a familiar set of wheels – or perhaps a new acquisition from a nearby European gem – often arises. Italy, sharing a rich automotive heritage and geographical proximity, stands out as a popular origin country for vehicles entering Spain.

While importing a car within the European Union is significantly simpler than from a non-EU country, it's not without its bureaucratic nuances. This comprehensive guide from ImportEspana.com is designed to demystify the process of importing a car from Italy to Spain, providing English-speaking expats and foreigners with all the practical information, step-by-step instructions, and cost breakdowns you'll need for a smooth and successful registration.

### Why Consider Importing a Car from Italy to Spain?

Italy, a fellow EU member state, offers several compelling reasons for sourcing your next Spanish vehicle. Understanding these advantages can help you decide if an Italian import is the right path for you.

#### The Allure of Italian Vehicles

Italy boasts a legendary automotive industry, famous for its style, performance, and engineering prowess. From iconic brands like Fiat, Alfa Romeo, and Lancia to luxury marques such as Ferrari and Lamborghini, Italian cars offer a unique blend of passion and design. Importing from Italy might allow you access to specific models, trims, or even better deals that aren't readily available or are more expensive in the Spanish market. Furthermore, the shared European automotive standards mean that Italian vehicles are generally well-suited for Spanish roads and regulations.

#### The EU Advantage: Simplified Procedures

One of the biggest benefits of importing a car from Italy (or any other EU member state) is the significantly streamlined process compared to non-EU imports. The principle of free movement of goods within the EU eliminates customs duties and complex import tariffs that would apply to vehicles from outside the bloc. While you'll still need to navigate Spanish registration and tax requirements, the absence of customs hurdles makes the overall journey much more manageable. You won't need an import license, and the vehicle will already meet European safety and environmental standards, simplifying the ITV (Inspección Técnica de Vehículos) inspection.

#### Potential Savings and Model Availability

Depending on the specific make, model, age, and condition of the car, you might find more competitive prices in Italy than in Spain. This is especially true for certain niche models or if you're looking for a specific used car that has a strong market presence in Italy. The sheer volume of cars available across the border can also increase your chances of finding the perfect vehicle that meets your budget and preferences.

### Essential Pre-Import Checks and Documentation from Italy

Before you even think about driving or shipping your car, meticulous preparation and securing the correct Italian documentation are paramount. This stage sets the foundation for a successful registration in Spain.

#### Finding Your Dream Italian Ride

Begin your search on reputable Italian automotive websites (e.g., AutoScout24.it, Subito.it) or through authorized dealerships. Private sales can offer better deals but come with increased risk, so proceed with caution. Always arrange to view the car in person if possible, or have a trusted third party inspect it on your behalf.

#### Verifying the Vehicle's History and Condition

Before committing to a purchase, it's crucial to verify the car's history.
*   **VIN Check:** Obtain the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) and run a comprehensive check through services like CarVertical or AutoDNA. This will reveal critical information about accident history, mileage discrepancies, previous ownership, and if the car has any outstanding finance or theft records.
*   **Mechanical Inspection:** If possible, have an independent mechanic inspect the vehicle. This can save you significant costs down the line by identifying potential issues before purchase.
*   **Seller Legitimacy:** Ensure the seller is the legal owner of the vehicle. Cross-reference their ID with the details on the vehicle's registration documents.

#### The Purchase Process: What to Expect

Once you've found your car, you'll enter the purchasing phase.
1.  **Sales Contract (Contratto di Compravendita):** Always insist on a written sales contract. This document should clearly state the buyer's and seller's details, vehicle information (make, model, VIN, mileage), agreed-upon price, and date of sale. Ensure it's signed by both parties.
2.  **Payment:** Discuss payment methods. For larger sums, a bank transfer is generally safest. Avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
3.  **Transfer of Ownership:** In Italy, the transfer of ownership is typically handled at an ACI (Automobile Club d'Italia) office or a *Pratiche Auto* agency. The *Certificato di Proprietà Digitale* (Digital Certificate of Ownership) is updated, and the *Libretto di Circolazione* (Registration Certificate) is annotated.

#### Key Italian Documents You MUST Obtain

These documents are non-negotiable and absolutely essential for registering your car in Spain. Without them, you will face significant delays or even be unable to register the vehicle.

*   **Libretto di Circolazione (Vehicle Registration Certificate):** This is the primary registration document, similar to a V5C in the UK or a "Fahrzeugschein" in Germany. It contains all the technical specifications of the vehicle and ownership details. Ensure it's valid and the seller's name matches the document.
*   **Certificato di Proprietà Digitale (Digital Certificate of Ownership):** This document proves legal ownership. While digital, you should receive a printed receipt or confirmation of its transfer to your name.
*   **Fattura di Vendita (Sales Invoice) or Contratto di Compravendita (Sales Contract):** This proves the purchase and the price paid. It's crucial for calculating taxes in Spain. Ensure it's detailed and signed.
*   **Certificato di Conformità (COC):** This document certifies that the vehicle complies with European Union type-approval standards. It's issued by the manufacturer and contains essential technical data (CO2 emissions, engine type, weight, etc.) required for ITV and DGT registration.
    *   **New Cars:** If the car is new (less than 6 months old OR less than 6,000 km), it will definitely come with a COC.
    *   **Used Cars:** Most used cars manufactured for the European market will have a COC. If the seller doesn't provide it, you can usually request one directly from the manufacturer (e.g., Fiat Spain, Alfa Romeo Spain) for a fee (typically €100-€250). If a COC is unavailable (e.g., for very old or modified vehicles), you might need to obtain a *Ficha Técnica Reducida* from an authorized engineer in Spain, which is a more complex and costly alternative.

### Transporting Your Car from Italy to Spain

Once the purchase is complete and you have all your documents, you need to get the car to Spain. You have two primary options: driving it yourself or using a professional transport service.

#### Driving It Yourself: Pros, Cons, and Temporary Solutions

*   **Pros:** Cost-effective if you're already making the journey, allows you to immediately use the car upon arrival (with temporary plates).
*   **Cons:** Requires temporary Italian insurance and potentially temporary plates, adds mileage, potential for breakdowns or accidents during transit, can be a long drive.
*   **Temporary Plates/Insurance:** If the car's Italian registration is cancelled (which is common for export), you'll need to obtain temporary export plates (*targa provvisoria per esportazione*) and temporary insurance in Italy to drive it to Spain legally. This process can add an extra layer of bureaucracy and cost in Italy. Ensure your temporary insurance covers you for the full journey to your destination in Spain.

#### Professional Car Transport Services: Convenience and Cost

*   **Pros:** Hassle-free, insured transport, no additional mileage, ideal if you're not driving to Spain yourself or if the car isn't roadworthy.
*   **Cons:** Higher cost compared to driving yourself, takes time for delivery.
*   **Cost:** Professional transport from Italy to Spain can range from **€500 to €1,200**, depending on the distance, vehicle size, and type of service (open or enclosed carrier). Get multiple quotes and ensure the service is fully insured.

### Navigating Spanish Bureaucracy: The Registration Process

This is where the bulk of the Spanish administrative work happens. Be prepared for several steps, each requiring specific documentation and fees.

#### The ITV Inspection: Your Car's Health Check

Before you can register your Italian car in Spain, it must pass a technical inspection at an ITV station (Inspección Técnica de Vehículos). This is Spain's equivalent of an MOT or TUV.
*   **What's Checked:** The ITV verifies that your vehicle meets Spanish safety and environmental standards. They'll check lights, brakes, tires, emissions, chassis, and ensure all details on the *Libretto di Circolazione* and COC match the vehicle's physical characteristics.
*   **Where to Go:** Find your nearest ITV station online. It's advisable to book an appointment in advance.
*   **Documents Needed for ITV:**
    *   *Libretto di Circolazione* (Original Italian Registration Certificate)
    *   *Certificato di Conformità* (COC) or *Ficha Técnica Reducida*
    *   Proof of ID/Passport
    *   Proof of Address in Spain (e.g., *Empadronamiento*)
*   **Cost:** The cost of an ITV inspection varies slightly by region and vehicle type but typically ranges from **€40 to €80**.
